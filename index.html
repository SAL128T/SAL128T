What is a Linear City?

A Linear City is an urban planning concept where development is organized along a linear transportation corridor. Rather than expanding outward in all directions, the city grows along a single axis, with transportation systems, utilities, and development concentrated in a narrow band.

This innovative approach minimizes urban sprawl, reduces transportation times, and creates a more sustainable and efficient urban environment.

Key Features

Efficient Transportation

High-speed rail and public transit systems run along the central corridor, reducing commute times and traffic congestion.

Green Spaces

Abundant parks and green areas are integrated throughout the linear design, providing recreational spaces and improving air quality.

Mixed-Use Development

Residential, commercial, and industrial zones are strategically positioned to reduce travel distances and create vibrant communities.

Sustainable Energy

Renewable energy sources power the city, with infrastructure designed for maximum energy efficiency.

Benefits of Linear Cities

  • Reduced commute times and traffic congestion
  • Lower infrastructure costs with concentrated development
  • Minimized environmental impact
  • Enhanced public transportation efficiency
  • Improved accessibility to services and amenities
  • Preservation of natural landscapes around the city
  • Greater community connectivity
